# Client brief — Fynbos & Fern (pilot)

> **Status: disposable pilot subject.** Fynbos & Fern is a realistic-but-fictional client
> used to prove the Phase 5 pipeline end-to-end (design direction → create-site → seed →
> deploy). It is written exactly like a real intake brief and must be treated like one:
> nothing site-specific may leak into template or section code.

## The client

Fynbos & Fern is a two-person indigenous garden design studio in Cape Town (Noordhoek).
Lead designer Marli Brand (12 years, ex-Kirstenbosch horticulturist) and project manager
Sipho Ndlela. They design, install and maintain water-wise gardens built entirely from
fynbos and other indigenous Cape flora. Clients are residential homeowners in the southern
peninsula plus a handful of boutique guesthouses.

Today they have an Instagram account and a Gmail address. Work arrives by referral; they
lose the "I googled you and found nothing" prospects. They want a small, beautiful site
that ranks for water-wise/indigenous garden design in Cape Town and turns visitors into
consultation bookings.

## What they sell

1. **Garden design** — site visit, concept plan, planting plan (from R8 500).
2. **Installation** — their own crew plants and builds what was designed.
3. **Seasonal maintenance** — quarterly visits keeping fynbos gardens in shape.
4. **Water-wise retrofits** — converting thirsty lawns/exotics to indigenous, incl.
   greywater and drip irrigation.

Primary CTA everywhere: **Book a garden consultation** (R650, credited against any
design fee — this offer must appear near every CTA). Secondary: browse the portfolio.

## Voice & feel

Warm, grounded, quietly expert. "We plant what belongs here." Earthy and textured, not
corporate, not eco-preachy. They love: protea/restio silhouettes, Cape granite, weathered
timber. They hate: glossy stock photos, lime "eco" green, anything that looks like an
irrigation company.

Brand assets supplied: none usable (Instagram photos only). Palette and type are the
design direction's job. Logos exist for four associations/partners they want shown:
SALI (SA Landscapers Institute), Life Landscapes nursery, Kirstenbosch supplier
programme, Water Institute of SA.
